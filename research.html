Recombinant Silk Production

Silk combines exceptional strength and flexibility with biocompatibility and biodegradability, making it ideal for textiles, tissue engineering, and sustainable materials. We focus on designing novel recombinant silk proteins to meet the demands of modern materials science and biotechnology.

  • Design and characterize novel recombinant silk proteins with enhanced mechanical or biomedical properties suited for specialized applications
  • Genetically engineer microbial hosts for improved expression, yield, and assembly of these recombinant silk proteins

Chromoproteins

Chromoproteins offer biodegradable, renewable alternatives to synthetic dyes that harm the environment through toxic runoff and excessive water use. We engineer durable chromoproteins for sustainable coloration across textile, cosmetic, food, and paper industries.

  • Engineer chromoproteins with enhanced colorfastness and stability for industrial applications
  • Develop scalable microbial production systems for cost-effective chromoprotein manufacturing

Team Member: Sahiti

Plastic Upcycling

Plastic Upcycling

Plastic pollution is one of the most pressing environmental challenges of our time, with millions of tons of plastic waste accumulating in landfills and natural ecosystems each year. Conventional recycling methods are often inefficient, and many plastics are not recycled at all, leading to long-term ecological harm.

In our lab, we are addressing this challenge by developing sustainable plastic upcycling strategies. Our research focuses on converting non-biodegradable plastics such as polystyrene and polyethylene terephthalate (PET) into high-value bioproducts through a combination of chemical and biological processes. Using approaches that integrate depolymerization with microbial fermentation, we transform plastic-derived compounds into functional materials like biopolymers and platform chemicals. This work supports the advancement of a circular economy and provides innovative pathways for mitigating plastic waste.

Team Members: Bobby and Steve
